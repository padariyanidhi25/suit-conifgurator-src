How to measure

Chest width

Lay it out

Lay the completely buttoned jacket flat on a table face up with the collar up and the arms positioned upward. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten out excess fabric.

Measure across

Starting at the lowermost point of the left armhole, measure across to the same point on the right.

One last thing

Be sure to measure across at the widest point and avoid any slack on the measuring tape.

How to measure

Waist width

Lay it out

Lay the completely buttoned jacket flat on a table face up with the arms positioned upward. Use your hands to extend and flatten out excess fabric.

Measure across

Starting from the jacket's left edge, measure across at the midway point just below the first button.

One last thing

Be sure to avoid any slack on measuring tape.

How to measure

Shoulder width

Lay it out

Lay the completely buttoned jacket flat on a table face down with the collar down and arms positioned downward. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten out excess fabric.

Measure across

Beginning on the left side, where the top of the armhole seam meets the shoulder fold, measure across, stopping at the same point at the right side of the jacket.

One last thing

Be sure to measure across at the widest point and avoid any slack on the measuring tape.

How to measure

Sleeve Length

Lay it out

Lay the completely buttoned jacket flat on a table facing upwards with the collar down and arms straightened to their natural position. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten out excess fabric.

Shoulder to cuff

Beginning at the top of the armhole seam (at the middle point of the shoulder) measure straight down through the middle of the sleeve to the sleeve edge.

One last thing

Be sure to avoid any slack on measuring tape.

How to measure

Jacket Length

Lay it out

Lay the completely buttoned jacket flat on a table face down with the collar up and arms positioned downward. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten out excess fabric.

Collar to hem

Beginning at the center of the collar base (where the jackets back seam meets the collar) measure to the bottom hemline.

One last thing

Be sure to avoid any slack on measuring tape.

How to measure

Waist Width

Lay them out

Lay the completely buttoned & zipped trouser flat on a table face up, use your hands to gently extend and flatten out the fabric.

Measure & mark

Begin by measuring from the edge of the waistband, down to the 1cm/0.5in point. Make note of this point.

Measure across the waist

Starting at the left edge, measure the waistband at the 1cm point noted in step 2. Hold in the center at the 1cm point, then continue measuring to the other side. Be sure to follow the natural curve of the waistband.

One last thing

Be sure to measure across at the widest point and avoid any slack on the measuring tape.

How to measure

Upper Leg Width

Lay them out

ay the completely buttoned & zipped trouser flat on a table facing up and slightly spread the legs to expose the inseams. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten out the fabric.

Measure & mark

Begin by measuring from the crotch downward along the right inseam to the 15cm/ 6in point. Make note of this point.

Measure across the leg

Beginning at the left edge of the pant leg, measure across at the previously marked 15cm/6in point.

One last thing

Be sure to measure across at the widest point and avoid any slack on the measuring tape.

How to measure

Inside Leg

Lay them straight

Lay the trousers flat on their side.

Split the legs

Fold the top leg above the waistband, exposing the crotch seams. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten the fabric.

Crotch to cuff

Begin where the left and right inseams meet, measure downward along inseam to the edge of the hem.

One last thing

Be sure to measure across at the widest point and avoid any slack on the measuring tape.

How to measure

Foot Opening Width

Lay them out

Lay the trouser hem flat on a table. Use your hands to gently extend and flatten out the fabric.

Measure across

Measure the length across the hem.

One last thing

Be sure to measure across at the widest point and avoid any slack on the measuring tape.
